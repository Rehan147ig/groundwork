# INTERNAL AUDIT FINDING

**Owner:** Head of Corporate Banking
**Distribution:** Internal Audit, Executive Committee, Audit Committee of the Board

## Finding

Internal Audit's Q1 2026 review of the corporate loan approval workflow has identified a control weakness in the handling of waivers and amendments. In six instances reviewed in the sample period (Oct 2025 – Jan 2026), the documented evidence of credit-group approval predated the formal request from the borrower's relationship manager by 24–72 hours. While substantive review took place in all six cases and no individual approval was inappropriate on its merits, the sequencing means that the audit trail does not support an after-the-fact demonstration of the prescribed process.

## Root cause

The root cause is the informal practice of holding "pre-clearance" discussions between RMs and the Credit Group ahead of formal submission. The bank has not previously documented expectations about whether these informal discussions are properly part of the approval record.

## Recommendations

1. Update the credit policy manual (POL-001) to require that all approval evidence be timestamped at or after the formal RM submission;
2. Provide a reference template for RM submissions to standardize the format;
3. Provide refresher training to the Credit Group on the documented expectation.

## Management response

Management accepts the finding and the recommendations. Implementation target is 31 May 2026.
