## Recommendation

Approve a new $50,000,000 senior secured acquisition financing facility to support Stark Industries plc's contemplated acquisition of Hammer Robotics SA. Facility to be drawn in a single tranche at completion of the acquisition (anticipated Q2 2026), with a 5-year bullet maturity. Pricing SOFR + 275bps, stepping down to SOFR + 250bps on achievement of net leverage ≤ 2.5x for two consecutive quarters.

## Strategic context

Stark Industries is a Tier-1 client and one of the bank's most significant corporate relationships in the defense-technology segment. The proposed acquisition of Hammer Robotics — a competitor in the autonomous-systems segment — is strategically transformative and has been the subject of senior-level discussions since October 2025. Confidentiality is paramount; the target is currently listed and any leak prior to formal announcement would be material.

## Financial overview

Stark Industries FY25 (audited): revenue $4.1Bn, EBITDA $720M, net debt $1.1Bn. Post-acquisition net debt projected $1.65Bn, net leverage 2.1x. Free cash flow generation more than adequate to service incremental debt. Hammer Robotics standalone is loss-making but has strong IP and an order book with the US DoD that would be transformative for Stark's robotics division.

## Conflicts and disclosures

The bank has provided M&A advisory to Stark Industries on this transaction (separate engagement, Investment Banking Division). Information barriers between IBD and Corporate Banking have been maintained throughout; this credit decision is taken on public information plus the additional confidential disclosures provided by Stark with our information-barrier waiver dated 18 January 2026.

## Risk commentary

Principal risks: (i) regulatory clearance (CFIUS, EU merger control) — counsel's preliminary view is approval likely with limited remedies; (ii) integration risk — Hammer Robotics culture is meaningfully different; (iii) reputational risk if the deal collapses post-announcement.

Executive Credit Committee approval requested at the meeting of 5 February 2026. Documentation subject to NDA already in place with the borrower.
