## Decision

The application for an $8,000,000 working capital facility from Hammer Industries SA is **DECLINED**. The Relationship Manager will communicate the decision and offer to assist with an orderly transition to an alternative provider.

## Rationale

Three factors, individually concerning, are collectively dispositive:

1. **Financial deterioration.** FY25 (unaudited) shows revenue contraction of 23% versus FY24, EBITDA negative, working capital cycle deteriorating sharply. Management commentary attributing this to "transitional restructuring" is not supported by the underlying ratios. Cash position covers approximately 4 months of operating expenses absent further drawdown.

2. **Adverse media and regulatory exposure.** Multiple credible reports through Q1 2026 of ongoing investigations by US and Swiss authorities into (a) allegedly inflated export-control filings on dual-use technology shipments, (b) related-party transactions with offshore entities not disclosed in the FY24 audit. Compliance has independently assessed adverse media as material; see linked file COMP-AM-2026-007.

3. **Sector and strategic concerns.** Hammer Industries operates in a segment where the bank has been actively reducing exposure for two reporting periods. Onboarding new exposure here runs against the stated corporate strategy.

## Communication

Decision to be communicated in writing by 21 March 2026. No counter-offer of alternative product to be made.
