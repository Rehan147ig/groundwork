## Background

Hammer Industries SA is a Switzerland-domiciled industrial company. Onboarded as a corporate customer in 2018 with a standard cash-management product set. The relationship has been routine through 2023; from late 2024 the bank's compliance team has observed escalating concerns.

## Adverse media — material findings

Between October 2025 and February 2026 the bank has identified the following credible adverse-media items, individually triaged and collectively summarized here under restricted distribution:

1. **US export-control investigation.** Reports of an ongoing US Department of Commerce review of Hammer Industries' export filings on dual-use technology shipments. No charges filed at time of writing; the investigation has been confirmed via filings made by Hammer Industries in its own home jurisdiction.

2. **Swiss tax-authority review.** Independent reports of a Swiss federal tax office review of related-party pricing on intra-group transactions with offshore entities. Hammer Industries has declined to comment publicly.

3. **Senior departure.** The Chief Financial Officer departed in November 2025 with no successor announced for approximately six weeks. Public commentary attributed the departure to "differences over corporate governance," language that compliance treats as material.

## Compliance recommendation

This packet supersedes the routine 2026 refresh. Compliance recommends:
- Immediate enhanced monitoring of all transactional activity (already in place since November 2025);
- No new product onboarding pending resolution of the adverse-media items;
- Coordination with the Senior Credit team in the event of any new credit request (separately, an $8M working-capital request from this customer was declined — see CM-2026-005).

A formal exit assessment will be tabled at the Compliance Committee meeting of 18 March 2026.
