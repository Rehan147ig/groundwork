# LOAN PORTFOLIO REVIEW — Q1 2026

**SYNTHETIC — DEMO USE ONLY — NOT REAL BANK DATA**

**Period:** 1 January 2026 – 31 March 2026
**Prepared by:** Credit Risk Management
**Distribution:** Credit Group, Senior Credit Committee, Head of Corporate Banking, Internal Audit

## Headline

Corporate loan book at quarter-end stood at $4.8Bn (FY24 year-end: $4.6Bn), reflecting net new origination of $310M offset by $110M of routine amortization and prepayments. Average risk rating ticked from 4.3 to 4.4 — within the policy range of 3.5–5.0 and consistent with the bank's articulated risk-appetite.

## Watchlist movement

Two relationships migrated to the watchlist this quarter:
- **GlobalTech Solutions Inc.** (Customer 100450, RM Natasha R.). Migrated following a covenant breach on the existing revolver (waived in February). The recent $2M equipment loan approval (CM-2026-003) was reviewed and is consistent with the watchlist treatment.
- **Hammer Industries SA** (Customer 200300, RM Natasha R.). Migrated following adverse media (separately documented in KYC-CUST-200300). A new working-capital request was declined (CM-2026-005). Compliance has tabled an exit assessment.

One relationship migrated off the watchlist:
- **Acme Manufacturing Ltd.** Three consecutive quarters of stable performance and covenant compliance.

## Sector concentrations

Top-three sector concentrations: defense-technology 18%, retail-luxury 14%, healthcare-services 11%. Defense-technology concentration is elevated relative to the policy ceiling of 15% and is being managed down through participation and syndication rather than new-money refusal. The bank's recent re-commitment to Wayne Enterprises Inc. (CM-2026-004) is consistent with this strategy: the Wayne facility is being syndicated to bring our held position back within sector limits.

## Forward view

Q2 2026 origination pipeline of $480M is consistent with the FY26 plan; no policy concessions are anticipated. Credit Group recommends no change to risk-appetite parameters.
