# COVENANT WAIVER MEMORANDUM

**SYNTHETIC — DEMO USE ONLY — NOT REAL BANK DATA**

**Ref:** LR-2026-WAIVER-STARK
**Date:** 18 February 2026
**Re:** Stark Industries plc — Request for one-time waiver of capex covenant under Facility Agreement dated 9 May 2024

## Background

Stark Industries plc holds an $800M senior revolving facility from a syndicate of which the bank is a $200M committed participant. The facility contains a customary annual capex limit of $350M. As of 31 January 2026, capex year-to-date is $410M against the FY25 ceiling. Management has requested a one-time waiver retrospective to the FY25 covenant test.

## Rationale advanced by the borrower

The breach is attributable to two specific capex items not contemplated at the time of facility execution: (i) acceleration of a contracted-for production line into Q4 2025 from Q1 2026 at the request of a major customer; (ii) a one-time information-security investment recommended by the company's external advisors in response to a sector-wide threat advisory. Management's view is that both items are non-recurring and that ongoing capex remains within the policy ceiling.

## Credit Group view

The bank has independently verified the underlying invoices for the two cited capex items. The acceleration of the production line is supported by a customer contract reviewed by the RM team. The information-security investment is supported by an external advisor's letter that has been independently confirmed.

The financial position of the borrower is in all other respects strong — net leverage 1.2x against a 3.5x covenant, interest cover 11.8x against 4.0x. The bank's risk position is not adversely affected by the requested waiver.

## Recommendation

Approve the one-time waiver of the FY25 capex covenant breach with the following conditions: (a) management to provide a revised FY26 capex plan within 30 days; (b) future covenant tests to be unchanged; (c) waiver fee of $50,000 to be paid pro-rata by the borrower across the syndicate.

—Credit Group
