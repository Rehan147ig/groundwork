# CREDIT POLICY MANUAL — SUMMARY

**SYNTHETIC — DEMO USE ONLY — NOT REAL BANK DATA**

**Effective:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** Head of Credit Risk
**Distribution:** All staff

This document summarizes the bank's credit policy. It is intentionally accessible to all staff so that any colleague — RM, ops, branch — can refer to the underlying expectations without needing to consult the full policy manual.

## Risk-rating scale

The bank uses a 10-point risk-rating scale where 1 is the strongest credit and 10 is in default. Ratings 1–6 are investment-grade equivalents; 7–8 are sub-investment grade; 9 is impaired; 10 is in default.

## Approval authorities

| Risk rating | Up to $5M | $5M – $25M | Above $25M |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1–4 | Senior RM | Credit Group | Credit Committee |
| 5–6 | Credit Group | Credit Committee | Credit Committee + Executive |
| 7–8 | Credit Committee | Executive Credit | Executive Credit |
| 9–10 | Workout team only | Workout team only | Workout team only |

## Sector concentration limits

No single sector to exceed 15% of the corporate loan book at quarter-end. Sectors exceeding 12% are reviewed quarterly. The Head of Corporate Banking is the policy owner for sector-concentration discipline.

## Single-name concentration

No single relationship to exceed 5% of Tier 1 capital. Cross-relationship concentrations (parent + subsidiary aggregated) reviewed quarterly.

## Covenant standards

Standard covenants for corporate facilities: net leverage, interest cover, capex limit. Waivers require Credit Group approval up to one cycle; multiple waivers require Credit Committee.

## Watchlist procedures

Migration to the watchlist requires Credit Group endorsement. Watchlist accounts are reviewed monthly and discussed at the Senior Credit Committee quarterly.
