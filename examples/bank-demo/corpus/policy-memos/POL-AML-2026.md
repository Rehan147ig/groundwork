# AML POLICY UPDATE — 2026

**SYNTHETIC — DEMO USE ONLY — NOT REAL BANK DATA**

**Effective:** 1 February 2026
**Owner:** Money Laundering Reporting Officer
**Distribution:** Compliance, all front-office RMs, Internal Audit

This memo updates the bank's AML policy in line with the most recent regulatory guidance and three observations from the bank's own thematic reviews. It supersedes POL-AML-2024-REV3.

## Updated screening cadence

All customers classified as Enhanced or High-risk are subject to refresh on a 12-month cycle (previously 18 months for High). Standard-risk customers retain the 24-month cycle. The first ageing cohort under the updated cycle will refresh between March and May 2026; expected uplift in workload is in the region of 35% over the prior period.

## Adverse media thresholds

Adverse-media items previously triaged at the analyst level for non-material findings are now subject to a four-eye review at the Senior Compliance level. The change is in response to two adverse-media findings in 2025 that were under-classified at the analyst level and re-categorized as material on subsequent review.

## PEP refresh

PEP customers (Domestic and Foreign) are subject to additional verification of source-of-funds documentation at each refresh. Documentation older than 24 months requires a fresh attestation.

## Sanctions screening

The bank's sanctions-screening logic is moving from a daily batch to near-real-time during business hours, with overnight batch confirmation. Implementation date is 1 May 2026. RMs will see no change to their workflow; the change is operational.

## RM responsibilities

Every RM should ensure that:
- All assigned customers are within their refresh cycle;
- Material adverse-media findings are escalated to Compliance the same day they are identified;
- Customer contact for source-of-funds verification is initiated promptly when requested.
