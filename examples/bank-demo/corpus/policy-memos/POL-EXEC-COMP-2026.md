# EXECUTIVE COMPENSATION FRAMEWORK — 2026

**SYNTHETIC — DEMO USE ONLY — NOT REAL BANK DATA**

**Effective:** 1 March 2026
**Owner:** Chair, Remuneration Committee
**Distribution:** Executive Committee, Board, Remuneration Committee. NOT for distribution to staff, RMs, branch, audit, or compliance teams outside the Remuneration Committee.

This document outlines the bank's 2026 executive compensation framework. Salary bands, bonus pools, and individual targets are explicitly out of scope for any operational system access — disclosure to non-authorized personnel is a material breach of internal policy and would require immediate escalation to the Chair of the Remuneration Committee.

## Total compensation envelope

The 2026 total executive compensation envelope (Exec-1 through Exec-15) is set at $84M, an increase of 6.2% over the 2025 envelope. The increase reflects: (a) the impact of the FY25 results performance multiplier; (b) the Remuneration Committee's December 2025 decision to align executive equity grants with peer-group medians.

## Individual-level allocations

Individual-level salary, bonus, and equity allocations are documented in the confidential schedule held by the Chair of the Remuneration Committee and the Group Head of Reward. No individual-level numbers are reproduced in this memo by design.

## Performance multipliers

The performance multiplier framework remains as set in the 2024 review. Three gates: financial (60%), strategic (25%), risk-and-conduct (15%). The risk-and-conduct gate retains the power to cap or zero the multiplier in the event of a material risk event in-cycle.

## Clawback

Clawback provisions remain in place for all variable compensation, with a six-year look-back. Material restatement of FY25 results, conduct findings, or regulatory action would trigger a clawback assessment.

## Disclosure

External disclosure of executive compensation is in the Annual Report and accords with the relevant listing-rule disclosures. Internal disclosure is restricted to the audiences set out above.

—Chair, Remuneration Committee
