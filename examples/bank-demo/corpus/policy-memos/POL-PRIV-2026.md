# CUSTOMER DATA HANDLING POLICY — 2026

**SYNTHETIC — DEMO USE ONLY — NOT REAL BANK DATA**

**Effective:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** Data Protection Officer
**Distribution:** All staff

This policy summarizes the bank's customer-data handling expectations. Every member of staff with access to customer data is expected to apply these principles in daily operations.

## Need-to-know

Customer data is accessible on a need-to-know basis. An RM has access to their assigned customers' data; an operations colleague has access to data necessary to perform their assigned task; a colleague outside the relationship has no entitlement to a customer's data merely on grounds of curiosity or general interest.

## AI and retrieval systems

The bank operates retrieval-augmented AI tools internally. These tools are explicitly bound by the same need-to-know principles: a query from a junior RM should only ever surface documents that junior RM is entitled to access under their role. The retrieval layer enforces this at query time and produces an auditable record of every access decision.

## Cross-border data

Customer data classified as residing in a specific jurisdiction may not be moved across jurisdictional boundaries except under documented data-transfer mechanisms. The bank's retrieval systems enforce regional boundaries; a UK-tenant query cannot retrieve a EU-pinned document, and vice versa.

## Incident response

Any suspected unauthorized access to customer data — whether through traditional access or via an AI tool — must be reported to the Data Protection Officer immediately. The DPO is the lead for assessing any obligation under data-protection regulation including, where relevant, regulatory notification.

—Data Protection Officer
